语气副词 Mood / Tone Adverbs
Mood 竟然 jìngrán unexpectedly / to one's surprise
他竟然通过了考试！
Tā jìngrán tōngguò le kǎoshì!
He unexpectedly passed the exam!
这么难的题，她竟然全做对了。
Zhème nán de tí, tā jìngrán quán zuò duì le.
Such a difficult problem, yet she got them all right.
我们等了一个小时，他竟然忘了。
Wǒmen děng le yī ge xiǎoshí, tā jìngrán wàng le.
We waited an hour, and he actually forgot.
Mood 究竟 jiūjìng / 到底 dàodǐ after all / on earth / actually
Both express emphasis in questions or demands for a definitive answer. 到底 is more colloquial; 究竟 is slightly more formal.
你究竟想做什么？
Nǐ jiūjìng xiǎng zuò shénme?
What on earth do you want to do?
这件事到底是怎么回事？
Zhè jiàn shì dàodǐ shì zěnme huí shì?
What is this matter actually about?
他究竟去不去？
Tā jiūjìng qù bu qù?
Is he going or not, after all?
Mood 难道 nándào rhetorical emphasis — "could it be that...?"
难道你不知道吗？
Nándào nǐ bù zhīdào ma?
Don't you know? (rhetorical)
难道这是我的错？
Nándào zhè shì wǒ de cuò?
Is this somehow my fault? (rhetorical)
See also: Rhetorical Questions 反问句
Mood 千万 qiānwàn absolutely must (not) / by all means
你千万别迟到！
Nǐ qiānwàn bié chídào!
Whatever you do, don't be late!
出门千万要带钥匙。
Chūmén qiānwàn yào dài yàoshi.
Make sure to bring your keys when going out.
这件事千万不能告诉别人。
Zhè jiàn shì qiānwàn bù néng gàosù biérén.
You absolutely must not tell anyone about this.
Mood 确实 quèshí indeed / truly / really
这个问题确实很难。
Zhège wèntí quèshí hěn nán.
This problem is indeed very difficult.
他说的确实有道理。
Tā shuō de quèshí yǒu dàolǐ.
What he said truly makes sense.
Mood 只好 zhǐhǎo have no choice but / can only
没有公交车了，我只好走路回家。
Méiyǒu gōngjiāo chē le, wǒ zhǐhǎo zǒulù huí jiā.
There are no more buses, so I have no choice but to walk home.
他不来，我们只好自己去。
Tā bù lái, wǒmen zhǐhǎo zìjǐ qù.
He's not coming, so we can only go by ourselves.
钱不够，只好买便宜的。
Qián bú gòu, zhǐhǎo mǎi piányi de.
Not enough money, so I had to buy the cheap one.
Mood 差(一)点儿 chà(yì)diǎnr almost / nearly
我今天差点儿迟到了。
Wǒ jīntiān chàdiǎnr chídào le.
I almost was late today.
他差一点儿就忘了。
Tā chà yìdiǎnr jiù wàng le.
He nearly forgot.
Mood 才 cái only then / as late as / merely (emphasizes lateness or smallness — contrast with 就)
他十二点才睡觉。
Tā shí'èr diǎn cái shuìjiào.
He didn't go to bed until twelve.
只有多练习，才能提高。
Zhǐyǒu duō liànxí, cái néng tígāo.
Only with more practice can you improve.
Mood 并 bìng (before 不/没) actually not / by no means — rejects an assumption
这道题并不难。
Zhè dào tí bìng bù nán.
This question is actually not hard (contrary to what you think).
我并没有忘记你的生日。
Wǒ bìng méiyǒu wàngjì nǐ de shēngrì.
I certainly haven't forgotten your birthday.
Mood 正好 zhènghǎo just right / happen to / at exactly the right moment
你来得正好，我们正要吃饭。
Nǐ lái de zhènghǎo, wǒmen zhèng yào chīfàn.
You came at just the right time — we're about to eat.
这双鞋我穿正好。
Zhè shuāng xié wǒ chuān zhènghǎo.
These shoes fit me perfectly.
程度副词 Degree Adverbs
Degree 十分 shífēn very / extremely
他十分高兴。
Tā shífēn gāoxìng.
He is extremely happy.
这件事十分重要。
Zhè jiàn shì shífēn zhòngyào.
This matter is very important.
Degree 更加 gèngjiā even more / all the more
下雨以后天气更加凉快了。
Xià yǔ yǐhòu tiānqì gèngjiā liángkuai le.
After the rain, the weather became even cooler.
她的汉语说得更加流利了。
Tā de Hànyǔ shuō de gèngjiā liúlì le.
Her Chinese has become even more fluent.
Degree 稍 / 稍微 shāo / shāowēi slightly / a little
请稍等一下。
Qǐng shāo děng yīxià.
Please wait a moment.
今天稍微有点儿冷。
Jīntiān shāowēi yǒudiǎnr lěng.
It's slightly cold today.
Degree 尤其 yóuqí especially / particularly
我喜欢运动，尤其是游泳。
Wǒ xǐhuan yùndòng, yóuqí shì yóuyǒng.
I like sports, especially swimming.
北京的秋天很美，尤其是香山。
Běijīng de qiūtiān hěn měi, yóuqí shì Xiāngshān.
Beijing's autumn is beautiful, especially Xiangshan.
Degree 多么 duōme how (exclamatory) / what a
今天天气多么好啊！
Jīntiān tiānqì duōme hǎo a!
What wonderful weather today!
这个地方多么漂亮！
Zhège dìfang duōme piàoliang!
How beautiful this place is!
范围副词 Scope Adverbs
Scope 共 gòng / 一共 yígòng altogether / in total
我们班共有三十个学生。
Wǒmen bān gòng yǒu sānshí ge xuéshēng.
Our class has thirty students in total.
一共花了两百块钱。
Yígòng huā le liǎng bǎi kuài qián.
It cost 200 yuan altogether.
Scope 全 quán all / entirely
他的话我全听懂了。
Tā de huà wǒ quán tīng dǒng le.
I understood everything he said.
这些书我全看过。
Zhèxiē shū wǒ quán kàn guò.
I've read all of these books.
Scope 光 guāng only / merely / just
光说不做没有用。
Guāng shuō bù zuò méiyǒu yòng.
Just talking without acting is useless.
别光看手机。
Bié guāng kàn shǒujī.
Don't just stare at your phone.
Scope 仅 / 仅仅 jǐn / jǐnjǐn only / merely
他仅仅学了一年汉语。
Tā jǐnjǐn xué le yī nián Hànyǔ.
He has only studied Chinese for one year.
这仅是开始。
Zhè jǐn shì kāishǐ.
This is merely the beginning.
Scope 至少 zhìshǎo at least
你至少要学一个小时。
Nǐ zhìshǎo yào xué yī ge xiǎoshí.
You need to study for at least an hour.
他至少认识五百个汉字。
Tā zhìshǎo rènshi wǔ bǎi ge Hànzì.
He knows at least 500 Chinese characters.
时间副词 Time Adverbs
Time 按时 ànshí on time / punctually
请按时交作业。
Qǐng ànshí jiāo zuòyè.
Please hand in your homework on time.
他每天按时上班。
Tā měi tiān ànshí shàngbān.
He goes to work on time every day.
Time 从来 cónglái always / never (with negation)
他从来不迟到。
Tā cónglái bù chídào.
He is never late.
我从来没去过那个地方。
Wǒ cónglái méi qù guò nàge dìfang.
I have never been to that place.
Time 赶紧 / 赶快 gǎnjǐn / gǎnkuài hurry up / quickly
赶紧走吧，要迟到了！
Gǎnjǐn zǒu ba, yào chídào le!
Hurry up and go, we'll be late!
你赶快把作业做完。
Nǐ gǎnkuài bǎ zuòyè zuò wán.
Hurry and finish your homework.
Time 忽然 hūrán suddenly
天忽然下起雨来了。
Tiān hūrán xià qǐ yǔ lái le.
It suddenly started raining.
他忽然想起了一件事。
Tā hūrán xiǎng qǐ le yī jiàn shì.
He suddenly remembered something.
Time 将 / 将要 jiāng / jiāngyào will / be about to (formal)
飞机将要起飞了。
Fēijī jiāngyào qǐfēi le.
The plane is about to take off.
我们将在下周开会。
Wǒmen jiāng zài xià zhōu kāi huì.
We will have a meeting next week.
Time 本来 běnlái originally / at first
我本来想去，后来改主意了。
Wǒ běnlái xiǎng qù, hòulái gǎi zhǔyì le.
I originally planned to go, but later changed my mind.
这件事本来就不难。
Zhè jiàn shì běnlái jiù bù nán.
This matter was never difficult to begin with.
Time 已 yǐ already (written / formal equivalent of 已经)
时间已不早了，我们回家吧。
Shíjiān yǐ bù zǎo le, wǒmen huí jiā ba.
It's already late — let's go home.
比赛已开始十分钟了。
Bǐsài yǐ kāishǐ shí fēnzhōng le.
The match has already been going for ten minutes.
频率副词 Frequency Adverbs
Freq 重新 chóngxīn again / anew / afresh
请重新写一遍。
Qǐng chóngxīn xiě yī biàn.
Please write it again from scratch.
他们重新开始了。
Tāmen chóngxīn kāishǐ le.
They started over.
Freq 往往 wǎngwǎng often / more often than not (habitual pattern)
春天往往下雨。
Chūntiān wǎngwǎng xià yǔ.
It often rains in spring.
不努力的人往往失败。
Bù nǔlì de rén wǎngwǎng shībài.
People who don't try hard often fail.
Freq 偶尔 ǒu'ěr occasionally / once in a while
我偶尔去那家餐厅吃饭。
Wǒ ǒu'ěr qù nà jiā cāntīng chīfàn.
I occasionally eat at that restaurant.
他偶尔给我打电话。
Tā ǒu'ěr gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
He calls me once in a while.
Freq 再次 zàicì once again / a second time
欢迎你再次来中国！
Huānyíng nǐ zàicì lái Zhōngguó!
Welcome to China once again!
Freq 老 lǎo always / keep on (colloquial, often with annoyance)
他老忘记带钥匙。
Tā lǎo wàngjì dài yàoshi.
He keeps forgetting to bring his keys.
别老玩手机！
Bié lǎo wán shǒujī!
Stop playing on your phone all the time!
方式副词 Manner Adverbs
Manner 故意 gùyì on purpose / deliberately
对不起，我不是故意的。
Duìbuqǐ, wǒ bú shì gùyì de.
Sorry, I didn't do it on purpose.
他故意把声音放低。
Tā gùyì bǎ shēngyīn fàng dī.
He deliberately lowered his voice.
Manner 互相 / 相互 hùxiāng / xiānghù mutually / each other
同学们应该互相帮助。
Tóngxuémen yīnggāi hùxiāng bāngzhù.
Classmates should help each other.
两国人民相互理解、相互尊重。
Liǎng guó rénmín xiānghù lǐjiě, xiānghù zūnzhòng.
The peoples of the two countries understand and respect each other.
关联副词 Linking Adverbs
Link 却 què but / yet — an adverb, so it must come AFTER the subject, before the verb
他个子不高，力气却很大。
Tā gèzi bù gāo, lìqi què hěn dà.
He isn't tall, yet he's very strong.
我等了他两个小时，他却没来。
Wǒ děng le tā liǎng gè xiǎoshí, tā què méi lái.
I waited two hours for him, but he didn't show up.
情态副词 Modal Adverbs
Modal 也许 yěxǔ perhaps / maybe
他也许忘了我们的约会。
Tā yěxǔ wàng le wǒmen de yuēhuì.
Maybe he forgot our appointment.
明天也许会下雨。
Míngtiān yěxǔ huì xià yǔ.
It might rain tomorrow.
Modal 恐怕 kǒngpà I'm afraid (that) / probably (an undesirable outcome)
现在出发，恐怕来不及了。
Xiànzài chūfā, kǒngpà láibují le.
If we leave now, I'm afraid it'll be too late.
他恐怕不会同意这个计划。
Tā kǒngpà bú huì tóngyì zhège jìhuà.
I'm afraid he won't agree to this plan.

Classic HSK 4 副词 Examples Explained / HSK 4 重点副词例句详解

12 high-frequency HSK 4 adverbs — 竟然 / 究竟 / 到底 / 难道 / 千万 / 确实 / 只好 / 差(一)点儿 / 仅仅 / 突然 / 居然 / 必须 — each with classic example, pinyin, English, and the typical mistake.

竟然 jìngrán — Surprisingly / 出乎意料

Marker for unexpected outcome. Indicates "I did not expect this!" — placed before the verb.

✓ 他竟然把我的钱包还回来了。 Tā jìngrán bǎ wǒ de qiánbāo huán huí lái le. — "He actually returned my wallet — I didn't expect that!"

Cousin word: 居然 (jūrán) — almost identical, slightly more spoken. 竟然 vs 既然 confusable pair →

究竟 jiūjìng — On earth / After all

Used in questions to demand clarity, often with frustration. Formal/written.

✓ 这件事究竟是怎么回事？ Zhè jiàn shì jiūjìng shì zěnme huí shì? — "What on earth is going on with this matter?"

Cousin: 到底 (dàodǐ) — same meaning, more spoken. 到底 vs 究竟 confusable pair →

到底 dàodǐ — In the end / On earth (spoken)

Like 究竟, expresses urgent demand for an answer. More common in spoken HSK 4 dialogues.

✓ 你到底想说什么？ Nǐ dàodǐ xiǎng shuō shénme? — "What on earth do you want to say?"

难道 nándào — Could it really be? / 反问

Rhetorical question marker — answer is opposite of the literal question. Pairs with 吗 at the end.

✓ 难道你不知道吗？ Nándào nǐ bù zhīdào ma? — "How could you not know?" (i.e., "you should know")

Pattern: 难道 + statement + 吗? The expected answer is the OPPOSITE of the literal question.

千万 qiānwàn — Be sure to / 务必

Strong warning marker, almost always with negative imperative 不要/别. Means "absolutely don't / be sure not to".

✓ 千万不要熬夜。 Qiān wàn bú yào áoyè. — "Whatever you do, don't stay up late."
✗ 千万要小心。 — Native speakers prefer 一定要小心. 千万 sounds most natural with negative commands.

确实 quèshí — Indeed / 的确

Confirms the truth of a statement. Often signals agreement.

✓ 这家饭店的菜确实好吃。 Zhè jiā fàndiàn de cài quèshí hǎo chī. — "This restaurant's food is indeed delicious."

只好 zhǐhǎo — Have to / 没有别的选择

"Have no choice but to" — implies the action is forced because alternatives have failed.

✓ 公交车坏了，我只好打车。 Gōngjiāo chē huài le, wǒ zhǐhǎo dǎ chē. — "The bus broke down, so I had to take a taxi."

差(一)点儿 chà (yì) diǎnr — Almost / Nearly

"Almost X happened." The actual outcome depends on whether X is something the speaker WANTED — this is the HSK 4 trap.

⚠ HSK 4 polarity rule (most-tested adverb point):

① UNWANTED action (迟到, 摔倒, 丢, 撞, 死, 哭):
• 差点儿 + V → action did NOT happen (lucky escape)
• 差点儿没 + V → also did NOT happen (essentially the same; 没 is intensifier)

② WANTED action (考上, 赢, 找到, 完成, 成功):
• 差点儿 + V → action did NOT happen (regret) ✗
• 差点儿没 + V → action DID happen (relief) ✓
✓ 我差点儿迟到了。 Wǒ chà diǎnr chí dào le. — "I almost was late." → I was NOT late (lucky). Unwanted action.
✓ 我差点儿就考上了。 Wǒ chà diǎnr jiù kǎo shàng le. — "I almost passed." → I did NOT pass (regret). Wanted action without 没 = did NOT happen.
✓ 我差点儿没考上。 Wǒ chà diǎnr méi kǎo shàng. — "I almost failed to pass." → I DID pass (relief). Wanted action with 没 = action DID happen.

Quick test: Ask "did the speaker want X?" — if YES, then 没 flips polarity; if NO, 没 makes no real difference. HSK 4 favorite trap is hiding 「差点儿没考上」 in a passage and asking whether the speaker passed (answer: yes, they passed).

仅仅 jǐnjǐn — Only / Merely

Emphatic "only" — slightly stronger than 只.

✓ 我们仅仅认识三个月就结婚了。 Wǒmen jǐnjǐn rènshi sān gè yuè jiù jiéhūn le. — "We had only known each other three months when we got married."

突然 / 忽然 tūrán / hūrán — Suddenly

Both mean "suddenly". 突然 can also be an adjective; 忽然 is adverb-only.

✓ 走着走着，突然下起雨来了。 Zǒu zhe zǒu zhe, tūrán xià qǐ yǔ lái le. — "While walking, it suddenly started to rain."

居然 jūrán — Surprisingly (spoken cousin of 竟然)

Same meaning as 竟然, slightly more conversational tone.

✓ 没想到他居然能说五种语言。 Méi xiǎng dào tā jūrán néng shuō wǔ zhǒng yǔyán. — "I didn't expect he could actually speak five languages."

必须 bìxū — Must / 一定要

Strong obligation. Often paired with 应该 / 一定 in HSK 4 reading.

✓ 学习汉语必须坚持。 Xuéxí Hànyǔ bìxū jiānchí. — "Learning Chinese requires persistence."

Comprehensive Quiz / 综合测试 (12 questions)

他通过了考试，大家都很惊讶。
竟然 expresses surprise at an unexpected outcome. Everyone was shocked that he passed, so "unexpectedly" fits perfectly.
没有公交车了，我走路回家。
只好 means "have no choice but to." No buses left, so the speaker had no option but to walk home.
你别把这件事告诉别人！
千万 emphasizes an urgent warning — "you absolutely must not tell anyone." It pairs with 别/不要 for strong prohibition.
我没见过这么美的地方。
从来 + 没 = "have never." I have never seen such a beautiful place. 从来 is used with negation to mean "never."
这件事是怎么回事？你得告诉我。
到底 demands a definitive answer — "What on earth is going on?" It conveys the speaker's insistence on knowing the truth.
我喜欢中国菜，是四川菜。
尤其 (是) means "especially." The sentence singles out Sichuan food from Chinese food in general.
Which adverb means "suddenly"?
忽然 (hūrán) means "suddenly." 赶紧 means "hurry up," 按时 means "on time," and 再次 means "once again."
今天有点儿冷，多穿一件衣服吧。
稍微 means "slightly." The sentence says it's a little cold, suggesting mild degree — not extremely cold (十分).
What is the difference between 竟然 and 居然?
竟然 and 居然 are near-synonyms meaning "unexpectedly/surprisingly." 竟然 is slightly more literary; 居然 is more colloquial with stronger disbelief. In most contexts they are interchangeable.
他忘记带钥匙，我都说他好几次了。
老 (colloquial) means "always / keeps on" with a sense of annoyance. The speaker is frustrated that he keeps forgetting his keys.
别看手机，也要看看书。
光 means "only/just" and implies doing one thing exclusively. "Don't just look at your phone, also read some books."
我想去北京，后来改去上海了。
本来 means "originally." I originally planned to go to Beijing but later changed to Shanghai. It marks the original intention before a change.

HSK 4 真题语境例句 / Real-Exam-Context Examples

Five sentences featuring frequently-tested HSK 4 adverbs in their natural exam contexts. Each shows the adverb's position rule and the meaning the test most often probes.

这次考试我差不多每道题都做完了。
Zhè cì kǎoshì wǒ chàbuduō měi dào tí dōu zuò wán le.
I finished almost every question on this test.
Test context: 听力 study/exam dialogues. 差不多 + 每…都 = "almost all"; 差不多 must precede the noun phrase, not the verb. Common HSK 4 选词 trap: students wrongly place 差不多 after 每.
你到底是怎么找到这家小饭馆的？
Nǐ dàodǐ shì zěnme zhǎodào zhè jiā xiǎo fànguǎn de?
How on earth did you find this little restaurant?
Test context: 听力 conversational questions. 到底 only appears in questions to push for a real answer; pairs with 是…的 to emphasize manner. HSK 4 frequently distinguishes 到底 (questioning) from 终于 (finally).
这个问题确实太简单了，连小孩子都会做。
Zhège wèntí quèshí tài jiǎndān le, lián xiǎo háizi dōu huì zuò.
This question really is way too easy — even kids can do it.
Test context: 阅读 with subjective evaluations. 确实 strengthens 太…了, often paired with 连…都 for double emphasis. HSK 4 frequently tests 确实 vs 其实 (确实 = "indeed, as expected"; 其实 = "actually, contrary to expectation").
他几乎每个周末都去图书馆学习。
Tā jīhū měi gè zhōumò dōu qù túshūguǎn xuéxí.
He goes to the library to study almost every weekend.
Test context: 阅读 character-description passages. 几乎 + 每…都 expresses high frequency without claiming totality — softer than 总是. Position rule: 几乎 sits before the time expression, not before the verb.
我们正聊得高兴，外面突然下起了大雨。
Wǒmen zhèng liáo de gāoxìng, wàimiàn tūrán xià qǐ le dà yǔ.
Just as we were chatting happily, it suddenly started pouring outside.
Test context: 阅读 narrative passages. 正…，突然… is a stock HSK 4 narrative frame for an unexpected interruption. 突然 must precede the verb (突然下起 ✓, *下突然起 ✗).
