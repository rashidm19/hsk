HSK 4 介绍传统饮食文化 — Traditional Food Culture

Understand introductions to traditional Chinese food culture — table manners, the meaning behind certain dishes, regional flavors, and time-honored shops and brands — and write a short paragraph introducing a food tradition.

Official Syllabus Requirement / 大纲要求

能听懂朋友、同学、老师等对中国传统饮食观念、中国各地饮食特点、传统店铺、品牌等中国传统饮食文化相关情况的有一定复杂度的介绍。如中国人的餐桌礼仪、某种食物的内涵，各地饮食的风味等。

Scenario Dialogue / 情景对话

Explaining dumplings and food customs to a foreign friend. Read it aloud twice: once for meaning, once for fluency — the syllabus tests this task across listening and speaking.

A
为什么过年一定要吃饺子呢？
Wèishénme guònián yídìng yào chī jiǎozi ne?
Why must people eat dumplings at Chinese New Year?
B
饺子的样子像古代的钱，代表新的一年发财，全家一起包更热闹。
Jiǎozi de yàngzi xiàng gǔdài de qián, dàibiǎo xīn de yì nián fācái, quánjiā yìqǐ bāo gèng rènao.
Dumplings look like ancient money, symbolizing wealth for the new year — and making them together is part of the fun.
A
我听说不同地方的口味差别很大？
Wǒ tīngshuō bùtóng dìfang de kǒuwèi chābié hěn dà?
I heard flavors differ a lot from region to region?
B
对，南方人爱吃甜的，四川人无辣不欢，北方菜偏咸。
Duì, nánfāng rén ài chī tián de, Sìchuān rén wú là bù huān, běifāng cài piān xián.
Yes — southerners like sweet, Sichuan people can't live without spice, and northern food tends salty.
A
"民以食为天"，中国的饮食文化真是博大精深。
"Mín yǐ shí wéi tiān", Zhōngguó de yǐnshí wénhuà zhēnshi bódà-jīngshēn.
"Food is heaven to the people" — Chinese food culture is truly broad and profound.

Why HSK 4 Candidates Struggle with Traditional Food Culture

Understand introductions to traditional Chinese food culture — table manners, the meaning behind certain dishes, regional flavors, and time-honored shops and brands — and write a short paragraph introducing a food tradition.

The biggest challenge for HSK 4 candidates on the traditional food culture topic isn't memorizing the 26 core words — it's understanding how they combine in extended contexts. HSK 4 traditional food culture questions typically require you to understand cause and effect, opinion shifts, or comparative judgements, not just basic vocabulary recognition.

Key vocabulary to anchor your understanding: 白酒 (báijiǔ, Chinese white liquor), 茶叶 (cháyè, tea leaves), 厨师 (chúshī, cook), 丰富 (fēngfù, rich). These words frequently appear in HSK 4 listening dialogues (听力) and reading passages (阅读), often with grammar patterns like comparison and measure-words.

Traditional Food Culture FAQ / 常见问题

How many HSK 4 words cover traditional food culture?

The HSK 4 official syllabus has 26 core words specifically for the traditional food culture task scenario. These come from 1 sub-topic categories: food-culture. Mastering these 26 words gives you 70-80% comprehension on traditional food culture-themed questions in HSK 4.

Which HSK 4 grammar points are most relevant to traditional food culture?

comparison and measure-words appear most frequently in traditional food culture contexts. The HSK 4 syllabus expects you to not only understand but also produce these patterns when they involve traditional food culture vocabulary.

What's the difference between HSK 3 and HSK 4 expectations on traditional food culture?

HSK 3 expects simple statements about traditional food culture (e.g., basic facts and short descriptions). HSK 4 raises the bar to "有一定复杂度" — handling extended contexts with opinions, comparisons, and reasoning. You'll need to express feelings (感受) and views (看法) about traditional food culture, not just describe them.
