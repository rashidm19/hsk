HSK 4 更 vs 还

even more (degree) vs also/still (addition)

Comparison / 对比

更 gèng还 hái
更Even more: 更好, 比…更…Intensifies comparison
还Still: 还在; also: 还有Surprisingly: 比他还高
Key diff更 = higher degree还 = addition or surprise
With 比比A更B = even more B than A比A还B = even more B (surprising)

Examples / 例句

更: 今天比昨天更冷了。
Jīntiān bǐ zuótiān gèng lěng le.
Today is even colder than yesterday.
还: 他会说中文，还会说日语。/ 我也想去。
Tā huì shuō zhōngwén, hái huì shuō rìyǔ. / Wǒ yě xiǎng qù.
He speaks Chinese and also Japanese. / I also want to go.
Quick rule: 更 = EVEN MORE (intensifies). 还 = ALSO/STILL (adds). With 比: 更 emphasizes degree, 还 implies surprise.

更 vs 还 FAQ

Are 更 and 还 interchangeable in HSK 4?

No. Although 更 (gèng) and 还 (hái) translate similarly into English, HSK 4 fill-in-the-blank and listening questions test exactly the distinction between them. 更 = EVEN MORE (intensifies).

How is 更 vs 还 tested in HSK 4?

Most often in HSK 4 阅读 (reading) Part 1 选词填空 (Q46-55), where you choose the correct word for a blank in a sentence. Also appears in 听力 (listening) where the speaker uses one but the printed answer paraphrases with the other. Master both 更 and 还 collocations to lock in these points.

What's the quickest way to remember 更 vs 还?

更 = EVEN MORE (intensifies). 还 = ALSO/STILL (adds). With 比: 更 emphasizes degree, 还 implies surprise. Practice 3-5 fill-in-blank questions in our HSK 4 mock exams to lock the distinction.
