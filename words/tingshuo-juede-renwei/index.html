HSK 4 听说 vs 觉得/认为

heard that vs think/believe — hearsay vs opinion

Comparison / 对比

听说 tīngshuō觉得/认为 juéde/rènwéi
听说I heard (secondhand)No personal judgment
觉得I think/feel (casual)Subjective opinion
认为I believe (formal)Strong assertion
SourceOthers told meMy own thinking

Examples / 例句

听说: 听说他要去美国留学。
Tīngshuō tā yào qù Měiguó liúxué.
I heard he is going to study in the US.
觉得/认为: 我觉得这个主意不错。/ 我认为应该改变计划。
Wǒ juéde zhège zhǔyì bùcuò. / Wǒ rènwéi yīnggāi gǎi biàn jìhùa.
I think this idea is good. / I believe we should change the plan.
Quick rule: 听说 = someone else said. 觉得 = your casual feeling. 认为 = your formal considered view.

听说 vs 觉得/认为 FAQ

Are 听说 and 觉得/认为 interchangeable in HSK 4?

No. Although 听说 (tīngshuō) and 觉得/认为 (juéde/rènwéi) translate similarly into English, HSK 4 fill-in-the-blank and listening questions test exactly the distinction between them. 听说 = someone else said.

How is 听说 vs 觉得/认为 tested in HSK 4?

Most often in HSK 4 阅读 (reading) Part 1 选词填空 (Q46-55), where you choose the correct word for a blank in a sentence. Also appears in 听力 (listening) where the speaker uses one but the printed answer paraphrases with the other. Master both 听说 and 觉得/认为 collocations to lock in these points.

What's the quickest way to remember 听说 vs 觉得/认为?

听说 = someone else said. 觉得 = your casual feeling. 认为 = your formal considered view. Practice 3-5 fill-in-blank questions in our HSK 4 mock exams to lock the distinction.
