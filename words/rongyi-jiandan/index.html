HSK 4 容易 vs 简单

easy (to do) vs simple (not complex)

Comparison / 对比

容易 róngyì简单 jiǎndān
FocusEasy to do / likely to happenSimple, not complicated
Structure容易 + verb: 容易感冒简单 + noun: 简单的问题
Opposite难 (hard to do)复杂 (complex)
Special useProne to: 容易生气 (easily gets angry)No 'prone to' usage

Examples / 例句

容易: 冬天容易感冒，要多喝热水。
Dōngtiān róngyì gǎnmào, yào duō hē rè shuǐ.
It's easy to catch a cold in winter. (prone to / likely to)
简单: 这道题很简单，大家都会做。
Zhè dào tí hěn jiǎndān, dàjiā dōu huì zuò.
This question is simple — everyone can do it. (not complex)
Quick rule: 容易 = easy to DO (verb follows) or likely/prone to. 简单 = simple, not complicated (describes a thing). You can say 这道题很简单 AND 这道题很容易, but only 容易 works in 容易感冒 (prone to catching cold).

容易 vs 简单 FAQ

Are 容易 and 简单 interchangeable in HSK 4?

No. Although 容易 (róngyì) and 简单 (jiǎndān) translate similarly into English, HSK 4 fill-in-the-blank and listening questions test exactly the distinction between them. 容易 = easy to DO (verb follows) or likely/prone to.

How is 容易 vs 简单 tested in HSK 4?

Most often in HSK 4 阅读 (reading) Part 1 选词填空 (Q46-55), where you choose the correct word for a blank in a sentence. Also appears in 听力 (listening) where the speaker uses one but the printed answer paraphrases with the other. Master both 容易 and 简单 collocations to lock in these points.

What's the quickest way to remember 容易 vs 简单?

容易 = easy to DO (verb follows) or likely/prone to. 简单 = simple, not complicated (describes a thing). You can say 这道题很简单 AND 这道题很容易, but only 容易 works in 容易感冒 (prone to catching cold). Practice 3-5 fill-in-blank questions in our HSK 4 mock exams to lock the distinction.
