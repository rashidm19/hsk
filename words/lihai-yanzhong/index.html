HSK 4 厉害 vs 严重

severe/impressive vs serious/grave — positive vs negative

Comparison / 对比

厉害 lìhai严重 yánzhòng
ToneCan be positive OR negativeAlways negative
Positive你太厉害了！(You're amazing!)Cannot be used as praise
Negative头疼得厉害 (terrible headache)污染很严重 (serious pollution)
ObjectsPeople, skills, pain, weatherProblems, situations, illness, damage

Examples / 例句

厉害: 她中文说得太厉害了！/ 外面风刮得很厉害。
Tā zhōngwén shuō de tài lìhai le! / Wàimiàn fēng guā de hěn lìhai.
Her Chinese is amazing! / The wind outside is fierce. (positive + negative uses)
严重: 空气污染问题越来越严重了。
Kōngqì wūrǎn wèntí yuè lái yuè yánzhòng le.
Air pollution is getting more and more serious. (always negative)
Quick rule: 厉害 is versatile — compliment (你真厉害!) or describe severity (疼得厉害). 严重 is ONLY for bad situations (严重的问题/污染/错误). Never say ×你真严重 as a compliment.

厉害 vs 严重 FAQ

Are 厉害 and 严重 interchangeable in HSK 4?

No. Although 厉害 (lìhai) and 严重 (yánzhòng) translate similarly into English, HSK 4 fill-in-the-blank and listening questions test exactly the distinction between them. 厉害 is versatile — compliment (你真厉害!) or describe severity (疼得厉害).

How is 厉害 vs 严重 tested in HSK 4?

Most often in HSK 4 阅读 (reading) Part 1 选词填空 (Q46-55), where you choose the correct word for a blank in a sentence. Also appears in 听力 (listening) where the speaker uses one but the printed answer paraphrases with the other. Master both 厉害 and 严重 collocations to lock in these points.

What's the quickest way to remember 厉害 vs 严重?

厉害 is versatile — compliment (你真厉害!) or describe severity (疼得厉害). 严重 is ONLY for bad situations (严重的问题/污染/错误). Never say ×你真严重 as a compliment. Practice 3-5 fill-in-blank questions in our HSK 4 mock exams to lock the distinction.
