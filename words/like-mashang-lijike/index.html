HSK 4 立刻 vs 马上

At once (formal) vs right away (spoken) — both 'immediately' with different feel

Comparison / 对比

立刻 lìkè马上 mǎshàng
RegisterFormal, written, news, instructionsSpoken, everyday, informal
Time gapAlmost simultaneously (0 seconds)Very soon (a few seconds to minutes)
Common use立刻去办 / 立刻通知 / 立刻执行马上来 / 马上回家 / 马上就好
Spoken ChineseLess common in casual chatMost common 'immediately' word

Examples / 例句

立刻: 接到通知后，警察立刻赶到了现场。
Jiē dào tōngzhī hòu, jǐngchá lìkè gǎn dào le xiànchǎng.
Upon receiving notification, the police immediately rushed to the scene.
马上: 你等一下，我马上就来。
Nǐ děng yíxià, wǒ mǎshàng jiù lái.
Wait a moment, I'll be right there.
Quick rule: 立刻 = strictly 'at once', formal tone, often in news/orders/business. 马上 = 'right away', everyday spoken Chinese, slightly more flexible (can mean 'in a few minutes'). For HSK 4: use 马上 in dialogue questions and casual contexts; use 立刻 in passages about formal action. 立即 (lìjí) is even more formal — almost identical to 立刻 but less common in HSK 4.

Quick Quiz / 小测验

妈妈，饭好了，我下楼。
Family conversation, casual = 马上. 立刻 sounds too formal in domestic dialogue.
公司决定停止这个项目。
Formal company decision = 立刻. Both technically work but 立刻 fits the formal register.
你别走，给我打电话。
Casual instruction to friend = 马上. 立刻 would sound bossy/cold.

立刻 vs 马上 FAQ

Are 立刻 and 马上 interchangeable in HSK 4?

No. Although 立刻 (lìkè) and 马上 (mǎshàng) translate similarly into English, HSK 4 fill-in-the-blank and listening questions test exactly the distinction between them. 立刻 = strictly 'at once', formal tone, often in news/orders/business.

How is 立刻 vs 马上 tested in HSK 4?

Most often in HSK 4 阅读 (reading) Part 1 选词填空 (Q46-55), where you choose the correct word for a blank in a sentence. Also appears in 听力 (listening) where the speaker uses one but the printed answer paraphrases with the other. Master both 立刻 and 马上 collocations to lock in these points.

What's the quickest way to remember 立刻 vs 马上?

立刻 = strictly 'at once', formal tone, often in news/orders/business. 马上 = 'right away', everyday spoken Chinese, slightly more flexible (can mean 'in a few minutes'). For HSK 4: use 马上 in dialogue questions and casual contexts; use 立刻 in passages about formal action. 立即 (lìjí) is even more formal — almost identical to 立刻 but less common in HSK 4.
