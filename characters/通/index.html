How to write 通 (tōng) — Stroke Order, Radical & Practice

通
tōng to pass through, to open, to connect; to communicate; common 10 strokes · Radical 辶 (to walk) · HSK 4 required writing character
Quick Answer

通 (tōng) means to pass through, to open, to connect; to communicate; common. It is written in 10 strokes, with the radical 辶 (to walk), and is one of the 150 HSK 4 required writing characters (rank #5 by appearance in HSK 4 vocabulary).

Pronunciation & meaning

Pronounced tōng.

  • to pass through
  • to open
  • to connect
  • to communicate
  • common

Decomposition

通 breaks down into 2 components arranged as Lower-left envelops Inner. Recognizing the components makes the character easier to remember and write.

通 =
辶 outer to walk
甬 inner path

Radical

The radical of 通 is 辶 — meaning to walk. Radicals are the indexing components used in Chinese dictionaries; they often hint at a character's broad meaning category.

Other HSK 4 characters that share this radical: 达连.

How the character is built

通 is a phono-semantic compound. The semantic component 辶 (walk) carries the meaning, while the phonetic component 甬 originally indicated the sound.
