How to write 父 (fù) — Stroke Order, Radical & Practice

父
fù father, dad 4 strokes · Radical 父 (father) · HSK 4 required writing character
Quick Answer

父 (fù) means father, dad. It is written in 4 strokes, with the radical 父 (father), and is one of the 150 HSK 4 required writing characters (rank #23 by appearance in HSK 4 vocabulary).

Pronunciation & meaning

Pronounced fù.

  • father
  • dad

Decomposition

父 breaks down into 2 components arranged as Top + Bottom. Recognizing the components makes the character easier to remember and write.

父 =
八 top eight
乂 bottom to govern

Radical

The radical of 父 is 父 — meaning father. Radicals are the indexing components used in Chinese dictionaries; they often hint at a character's broad meaning category.

How the character is built

父 is an ideographic compound — its meaning is suggested by the combination of its parts rather than by sound. A hand 乂 holding an axe 八.

HSK 4 Words Containing 父

父母 fùmǔ parents; father and mother
父母总是担心孩子的安全。
Fùmǔ zǒngshì dānxīn háizi de ānquán.
Parents always worry about their children's safety.
父女 fùnǚ father and daughter
他们是一对父女。
Tāmen shì yí duì fùnǚ.
They are a father and daughter.
父亲 fùqīn father
我的父亲是一名工程师。
Wǒ de fùqīn shì yì míng gōngchéngshī.
My father is an engineer.
父子 fùzǐ father and son
这对父子长得很像。
Zhè duì fùzǐ zhǎng de hěn xiàng.
This father and son look very alike.
