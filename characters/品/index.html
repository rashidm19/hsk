How to write 品 (pǐn) — Stroke Order, Radical & Practice

品
pǐn article, good, product; commodity; quality, character 9 strokes · Radical 口 (mouth) · HSK 4 required writing character
Quick Answer

品 (pǐn) means article, good, product; commodity; quality, character. It is written in 9 strokes, with the radical 口 (mouth), and is one of the 150 HSK 4 required writing characters (rank #29 by appearance in HSK 4 vocabulary).

Pronunciation & meaning

Pronounced pǐn.

  • article
  • good
  • product
  • commodity
  • quality
  • character

Decomposition

品 breaks down into 3 components arranged as Top + Bottom. Recognizing the components makes the character easier to remember and write.

品 =
口 top mouth
口 bottom mouth
口 part mouth

Radical

The radical of 品 is 口 — meaning mouth. Radicals are the indexing components used in Chinese dictionaries; they often hint at a character's broad meaning category.

Other HSK 4 characters that share this radical: 吹台否另各味喊.

How the character is built

品 is an ideographic compound — its meaning is suggested by the combination of its parts rather than by sound. Something that everyone is talking 口 about.
