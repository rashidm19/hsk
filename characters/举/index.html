How to write 举 (jǔ) — Stroke Order, Radical & Practice

举
jǔ to raise; to recommend; to praise 9 strokes · Radical 丶 (dot) · HSK 4 required writing character
Quick Answer

举 (jǔ) means to raise; to recommend; to praise. It is written in 9 strokes, with the radical 丶 (dot), and is one of the 150 HSK 4 required writing characters (rank #21 by appearance in HSK 4 vocabulary).

Pronunciation & meaning

Pronounced jǔ.

  • to raise
  • to recommend
  • to praise

Decomposition

举 breaks down into 2 components arranged as Top + Bottom. Recognizing the components makes the character easier to remember and write.

举 =
兴 top to thrive
扌 bottom hand

Radical

The radical of 举 is 丶 — meaning dot. Radicals are the indexing components used in Chinese dictionaries; they often hint at a character's broad meaning category.

How the character is built

举 is a phono-semantic compound. The semantic component 扌 (hand) carries the meaning, while the phonetic component 舉 originally indicated the sound.

HSK 4 Words Containing 举

举 jǔ to raise; to hold up
请举手回答问题。
Qǐng jǔ shǒu huídá wèntí.
Please raise your hand to answer.
举办 jǔbàn to hold (an event); to host
这座城市将举办马拉松比赛。
Zhè zuò chéngshì jiāng jǔbàn mǎlāsōng bǐsài.
This city will host a marathon.
举例 jǔlì to give an example
老师举例说明了这个语法点。
Lǎoshī jǔlì shuōmíng le zhège yǔfǎ diǎn.
The teacher gave examples to explain this grammar point.
举行 jǔxíng to hold; to take place
毕业典礼将在下周举行。
Bìyè diǎnlǐ jiāng zài xià zhōu jǔxíng.
The graduation ceremony will be held next week.
