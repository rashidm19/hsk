How to write 停 (tíng) — Stroke Order, Radical & Practice

停
tíng to suspend, to halt, to delay; suitable 11 strokes · Radical 亻 (man) · HSK 4 required writing character
Quick Answer

停 (tíng) means to suspend, to halt, to delay; suitable. It is written in 11 strokes, with the radical 亻 (man), and is one of the 150 HSK 4 required writing characters (rank #15 by appearance in HSK 4 vocabulary).

Pronunciation & meaning

Pronounced tíng.

  • to suspend
  • to halt
  • to delay
  • suitable

Decomposition

停 breaks down into 2 components arranged as Left + Right. Recognizing the components makes the character easier to remember and write.

停 =
亻 left man
亭 right pavilion

Radical

The radical of 停 is 亻 — meaning man. Radicals are the indexing components used in Chinese dictionaries; they often hint at a character's broad meaning category.

Other HSK 4 characters that share this radical: 保任使倒低例俩份.

How the character is built

停 is a phono-semantic compound. The semantic component 亻 (person) carries the meaning, while the phonetic component 亭 originally indicated the sound.
