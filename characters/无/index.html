How to write 无 (wú) — Stroke Order, Radical & Practice

无
wú no, not; lacking, -less 4 strokes · Radical 无 (no) · HSK 4 required writing character
Quick Answer

无 (wú) means no, not; lacking, -less. It is written in 4 strokes, with the radical 无 (no), and is one of the 150 HSK 4 required writing characters (rank #17 by appearance in HSK 4 vocabulary).

Pronunciation & meaning

Pronounced wú.

  • no
  • not
  • lacking
  • -less

Decomposition

无 breaks down into 2 components arranged as Top + Bottom. Recognizing the components makes the character easier to remember and write.

无 =
一 top one
尢 bottom weak

Radical

The radical of 无 is 无 — meaning no. Radicals are the indexing components used in Chinese dictionaries; they often hint at a character's broad meaning category.

HSK 4 Words Containing 无

无 wú there is no; without; to lack
他无话可说。
Tā wú huà kě shuō.
He had nothing to say.
无法 wúfǎ unable to; no way to
这个问题目前无法解决。
Zhège wèntí mùqián wúfǎ jiějué.
This problem cannot be solved at present.
无聊 wúliáo boring; bored
周末一个人在家很无聊。
Zhōumò yí gè rén zài jiā hěn wúliáo.
It's boring being home alone on weekends.
无论 wúlùn no matter what; regardless
无论结果如何，我们都接受。
Wúlùn jiéguǒ rúhé, wǒmen dōu jiēshòu.
No matter what the result, we accept it.
