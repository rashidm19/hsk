HSK 4 Test-Taking Strategy

HSK 4 True/False / 判断对错

Catch the keyword shift in 8 seconds — the highest-yield 10 marks of HSK 4

Section: Listening Part 1 / 听力第一部分
Questions: Q1-10
Format: 10 short statements, played once

HSK 4 listening Part 1 plays a short statement followed by a written sentence. You judge whether the written sentence matches the meaning of what you heard. With only one play and 8 seconds per question, this is the section where good strategy gives you 7-8 of the 10 available marks.

5 Strategies for HSK 4 Judgement (T/F) / 5 个应试策略

1

Pre-read the written sentence in 5 seconds

Before each audio plays, scan the printed sentence and underline the key noun + verb + any number/time/negation. The audio is short — you must know what to listen FOR, not just listen.

Example Printed: 他对工作很满意。 → underline 工作, 满意. Listen for whether the speaker confirms 满意 or contradicts it (e.g., 不满意, 失望, 抱怨).
2

Watch for the 3 classic HSK 4 traps

(1) Negation flip: 不/没/没有 in audio that's missing in the printed line. (2) Subject swap: audio says '哥哥' but printed says '弟弟'. (3) Time shift: audio says 上周 but printed says 下周.

Example Audio: 我下周才有空。 Printed: 我这周有空。 → 错 (this week vs next week — a 时间陷阱).
3

If you hear a synonym, the answer is usually 对

Statements that match through synonyms are common. 漂亮↔好看, 高兴↔开心, 难过↔伤心, 经常↔常常, 立刻↔马上 all signal 对. Don't insist on exact word match.

Example Audio: 这件衣服真好看。 Printed: 这件衣服很漂亮。 → 对 (synonyms match).
4

Mood/attitude words decide many T/F items

Listen for 喜欢 vs 讨厌, 同意 vs 反对, 满意 vs 失望, 习惯 vs 不适应. The printed sentence often paraphrases the speaker's attitude, and a single mood word flip = 错.

Example Audio: 这部电影还可以。(neutral) Printed: 他非常喜欢这部电影。(very positive) → 错 (degree mismatch).
5

Don't think — guess and move on after 8 seconds

If you missed the audio, mark a guess (statistically 对 ≈ 53% in HSK 4 Part 1) and move on. Lingering loses you the next question too. Listening Part 1 is a ladder — never skip a rung.

Example Trapped on Q3? Mark 对, move to Q4. The next audio starts in 5 seconds and waits for nobody.

⚠ Common HSK 4 Mistakes to Avoid / 常见错误

  • Reading the printed sentence AFTER the audio plays — you'll only catch half the meaning.
  • Translating the audio mentally to your native language — you lose 2 seconds and miss the next word.
  • Marking 错 only when something feels 'off' — many 错 items have only one word swapped (a number, a name, a tense).
